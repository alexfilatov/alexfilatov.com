## The Two Killers

I've talked to hundreds of aspiring founders, and the same two problems come up every single time.

### Idea Paralysis

You have an idea, but you can't figure out if it's worth pursuing. Should you quit your job for this? Should you build an MVP first? Is the market big enough? Who are the competitors? What's the business model?

Every question spawns three more. You overthink. You second-guess. And eventually, the excitement that made the idea feel alive in the first place just... fades. Not with a bang, but with a slow, quiet death by indecision.

### Information Overload

So you google "how to validate a startup idea." You get 500 blog posts, 200 YouTube videos, and 50 online courses. Each one says something slightly different. One tells you to build an MVP immediately. Another says don't write a single line of code until you've done 100 customer interviews. A third says forget all that and just launch a landing page.

You're more confused than before you started. The irony of the golden age of free startup advice is that most aspiring founders are more paralyzed than ever.
